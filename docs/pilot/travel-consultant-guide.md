# Travel Consultant Guide

## Start in Operations

Open **Operations** at the beginning of the day. Work in this order unless agency policy says otherwise: urgent or breached deadlines, travel-critical blockers, unassigned work, due-soon follow-ups, then routine queue items.

## Core Operating Path

1. **Client and Passenger:** search before creating records; keep commercial ownership separate from traveller identity.
2. **Request:** capture the route, dates, passengers, service needs, priority, and missing information.
3. **Trip:** use the controlled request-to-trip mapping when the request is structurally ready.
4. **Offer:** prepare choices from linked context, separate internal notes from client wording, and preserve the accepted snapshot.
5. **Booking handoff:** review passenger/segment mapping, approvals, documents, pricing, and payment prerequisites.
6. **Booking:** record controlled metadata or imports; no supplier booking is executed.
7. **Ticket / EMD:** record document, coupon, fare, and service metadata only.
8. **Passenger services and Documents:** keep required, requested, received, reviewed, and verified states distinct.
9. **Finance:** link invoice and payment metadata to the correct operational records; no payment is processed.
10. **After Sales:** use canonical selectors and preserve ticket, EMD, accepted-offer, booking, service, invoice, and payment references.

## When Something Is Missing

Keep the record in a warning, blocked, or manual-review state. Add a task with an owner and due date where appropriate. Do not invent supplier confirmation or bypass a guarded transition.
