# Passenger Service Operations Manifesto

AeroAssist exists to fulfil passenger service requirements, not merely to sell tickets.

The operating unit of AeroAssist is the passenger service case. Flights, fares, tickets, EMDs, documents, approvals, and airline messages matter because they help a person complete a journey under real operational conditions.

## Core Beliefs

- Passenger need is the root object.
- Operational reality overrides software convenience.
- Evidence comes before recommendation.
- AI does not create operational truth; structured knowledge does.
- Capability is different from policy.
- Airline operations are conditional operational constraints.
- AeroAssist remains advisory; human authority remains final.

## Passenger Need

Passenger need is broader than origin, destination, and price. It includes mobility, medical, animal transport, extra-seat, baggage, document, approval, timing, connection, airport handling, aircraft, cabin, and airline-specific constraints.

A passenger service requirement should not be flattened into a generic note when it has operational consequences. It should become structured metadata that can be linked to evidence, constraints, capability, pricing, documents, timeline events, workflow stages, tickets, EMDs, and future decision packs.

## Operational Truth

Operational truth comes from reviewed evidence, canonical vocabulary, and structured airline knowledge. Free text can preserve context, but future recommendations must be grounded in reviewed and versioned operational records.

Airline rules are not universal. They vary by airline, route, aircraft, cabin, airport, approval status, document status, time window, passenger condition, service category, SSR/OSI handling, EMD requirement, fare condition, and operational exception.

## Advisory Boundary

AeroAssist should help staff see what is possible, what is uncertain, what requires evidence, and what needs human review. It must not silently replace airline authority, airport authority, medical authority, border authority, supplier authority, or trained agency judgement.

AeroAssist does not help people buy journeys. AeroAssist helps people complete journeys that would otherwise be difficult, uncertain, or impossible.
