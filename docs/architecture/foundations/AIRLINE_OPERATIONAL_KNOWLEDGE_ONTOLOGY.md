# Airline Operational Knowledge Ontology

The Airline Operational Knowledge Ontology defines how AeroAssist structures airline-side knowledge for AOIE.

## Hierarchy

Airline
-> Evidence
-> Policy
-> Pricing
-> Capability
-> Constraint
-> Procedure
-> Outcome

## Meaning

Airline is the operating or marketing carrier whose service conditions are being evaluated.

Evidence is the reviewed source that supports a knowledge claim.

Policy is the airline's stated rule or condition.

Pricing is the commercial charge, fee, fare, tax, EMD, refundability, or cost condition attached to the service.

Capability is what the airline, aircraft, cabin, airport, station, or operational pathway can support.

Constraint is the structured condition that limits whether, when, or how the service can be fulfilled.

Procedure is the operational step, approval path, document requirement, communication pathway, or handling process required to fulfil the service.

Outcome is the future advisory result: eligible, ineligible, requires review, requires approval, requires document, requires EMD, uncertain, risky, or another governed conclusion.

## AOIE Connection

AOIE connects the Passenger Service Ontology with the Airline Operational Knowledge Ontology.

Passenger need supplies the operational question. Airline operational knowledge supplies the evidence-backed answer space. AOIE should not invent truth from text or AI output; it should reason from reviewed evidence, canonical taxonomy, policy, pricing, capability, constraints, and procedures.
